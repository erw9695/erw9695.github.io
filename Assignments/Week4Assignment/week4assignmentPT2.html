Bringing us back to the present day, we see the relative reflecting on Amoo’s death. They point out the stark differences in how he was viewed as a soldier compared to how his family sees it. As a soldier, Amoo’s death was described with generalized terms used to refer to soldiers killed. But here Sharif is trying to contrast this by showing the significant impact it has on the family.

"You are what is referred to as
a "casualty." Unclear whether
from a catalytic or frontal attack, unclear

the final time you were addressed

thou, beloved. It was for us a
catastrophic event.

Just, destroyed.

died of wounds received in action.

Yes, there was
early warning.
You said you were especially scared
of mortar rounds."
